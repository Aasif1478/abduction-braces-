Congenital clubfoot (Talipes Equinovarus) is a birth deformity in which the foot
twists inward and downward. If untreated, it leads to severe mobility limitations
and lifelong disability. The standard treatment is the Ponseti method, involving
serial casting followed by prolonged use of an abduction brace.

Despite successful initial correction, relapse remains common due to poor brace
compliance caused by discomfort, rigidity, and lack of monitoring.
